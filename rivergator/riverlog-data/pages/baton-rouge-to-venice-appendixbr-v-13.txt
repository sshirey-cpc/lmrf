Rivergator Appendix 12: Definitions for Baton Rouge to Gulf of Mexico Baton Rouge - an old boundary marker on top of Istrouma Bluff Batture - land between the levee and the river Bayou - "Bayuk" in Choctaw = "Small Stream." Winding swampy waterway with cypress trees and little or no flow Bayou Goula - a Native American tribe from Mississippi and Louisiana. Translates as “bayou people.” Chickory - roasted and ground root of the sassafrass, New Orleans blend mixed with coffee Manchac - "Rear Entrance" in Choctaw, Manchac Bayou is an old trade route to Gulf Plaquemine - Bayou Goula word for "Persimmon" Roux - flour toasted in grease, starter for cajun specialties like gumbo and jambalaya
