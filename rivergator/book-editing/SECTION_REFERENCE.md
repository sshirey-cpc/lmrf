## St. Louis to Caruthersville (Mile 195-850)

### River Gauges & Water Levels

**Gauge Station:** St. Louis Gauge (SLG)

| Level | SLG Reading |
|---|---|
| Low Water | 4 to 12 SLG |
| Medium Water | 13 to 24 SLG |
| High Water | 25 to 30 SLG |
| Flood Stage | 30 SLG and above |
| Action Stage | 28 SLG |
| Moderate Flood Stage | 35 SLG |
| Major Flood Stage | 40 SLG |

**Flood Stage Warning:** Above 30 SLG -- stay off the river. Limited access, most landings underwater, no easy camping.

**Special Notes:**
- At 38 SLG the U.S. Coast Guard will consider closing the St. Louis Harbor to navigation.
- At 40 SLG the Harbor is closed to recreational boaters.

### Dike Scale (St. Louis Gauge)

| SLG | Dike Conditions |
|---|---|
| 10-12 | Water flowing through notches only |
| 13 | Rocks still exposed on all dikes |
| 14-15 | Dikes starting to go under, some flow through breaks & low spots |
| 16 | Dikes completely under, but little flow |
| 18-20 | Good flow and lots of boils & turbulence |
| 22 | Strong flow, some turbulence, no dikes exposed anywhere |
| 25 | River bank full |
| >30 | Flood Stage Warning |

**Note:** Some dikes have been "notched" -- paddlers can pass through the middle notch at much lower levels, some down to 0 SLG.

### Wild Miles

- **Mile 136 to 130** -- 6 Wild Miles above St. Genevieve (historic Fort DeChartres)
- **Mile 121 to 110** -- 11 Wild Miles, below St. Genevieve to above Chester (Middle Mississippi River NWR-Kaskaskia Island)
- **Mile 105 to 82** -- 23 Wild Miles, below Chester to above Grand Tower (NWR-Rockwood Island, Wilkinson Island)
- **Mile 80 to 54** -- 26 Wild Miles, below Grand Tower to above Cape Girardeau (Trail of Tears State Park)
- **Mile 45 to 8** -- 37 Wild Miles, below Cape Girardeau to above Cairo
- **Mile 935 to 925** -- 10 Wild Miles, below Columbus to above Hickman
- **Mile 920 to 890** -- 30 Wild Miles, below Hickman to above New Madrid (near Reelfoot Lake; frequent bald eagle sightings)
- **Mile 867 to 850** -- 17 Wild Miles, below Tiptonville to above Caruthersville Harbor (unbroken forests)

### Beginner Locations

- Ellis Bay
- Ste. Genevieve Harbor
- Mouth of the Meramec
- Mouth of the Kaskaskia

- Hickman Harbor
- Caruthersville Harbor

## Baton Rouge to Venice (Mile 229-10)

### River Gauges & Water Levels

No formal gauge table is provided for this section. At very low river stage, the Baton Rouge gauge shows a tidal signal. Below Baton Rouge, tides begin to exert influence (especially below New Orleans). Paddlers should monitor the NOAA Lower Mississippi River Forecast and local tide reports.

**Special Notes:**
- VHF Marine Radio switches to Channel 67 between Baton Rouge and the Gulf.
- Below Baton Rouge the river enters "Chemical Corridor" -- 200+ petrochemical plants in 135 miles.
- Freighter displacement can cause 2-foot water level changes with waves of 3+ feet. Camp at least 3 vertical feet above high tide.

### Dike Scale

No dike scale provided. Dikes are less relevant below Baton Rouge; the primary navigation hazards are fleeted barges, freighters, blind corners, and industrial wharfing.

### Wild Miles

Per wildmiles.org, the Lower Mississippi overall is 71% wild / 29% not. However, below Baton Rouge to the Gulf (250 miles) the ratio inverts: **7% wild, 93% industrial/developed.** The Rivergator identifies "Green Spaces" -- pockets of wilderness between industrial stretches -- and describes them in detail, starting with Manchac Point.

### Beginner Locations

Not applicable. This section is for expert paddlers only. The three remaining islands below Baton Rouge (Plaquemine Island, Bayou Goula Island, Bonnet Carre Island) are the last islands on the Lower Mississippi.

### Guide Services

- **Paul Orr / Michael Orr** (Lower Mississippi Riverkeeper) -- Shuttles, resupplies, parking, logistics from St. Francisville to the Gulf. Paul: paul@lmrk.org, (225) 300-3902. Michael: michael@leanweb.org, (225) 803-2999.
- **USCG Sector New Orleans** (emergency): 504-846-5923

## Birdsfoot Delta (Mile 10-0)

### River Gauges & Water Levels

**Gauge Station:** Venice Gauge (VG)

| Level | Venice Gauge |
|---|---|
| Low Water | 0 to 1 VG |
| Medium Water | 1 to 2 VG |
| High Water | 2 to 4 VG |
| Bank Full | 1 VG |
| Flood Stage | 4 VG and above |

**Flood Stage Warning:** Above 4 VG (at slack tide) -- stay off the water. Fast and turbulent conditions, very limited access, all sandbars and ocean spits underwater.

**Special Notes:**
- Best time to paddle to the Gulf is during low/medium water (0-2 VG), usually late summer and fall.
- Record low: -0.77 VG (Christmas 1989). Record high: 9.11 VG (Hurricane Camille, August 1969).
- Tides normally vary 0.5 to 1 foot; can reach 2 feet in highest annual tides. Winds and storms can add up to 5 feet at the ends of passes, up to 9 feet at Venice.
- A high tide forecast of 3 feet or more would likely put all campsites below Venice underwater.

### Dike Scale

Not applicable. No wing dam/dike system in the Birdsfoot Delta. Navigation hazards are tidal, weather-driven, and traffic-related (freighters in Southwest Pass).

### Wild Miles

Not applicable. The Birdsfoot Delta is the terminus of the river. The landscape is marsh, passes, and open Gulf water. South Pass and Southeast Pass (within a State Wildlife Management Area) offer the most natural settings.

### Beginner Locations

Not applicable. Expert paddlers only. Open Gulf water, strong tidal currents, freighter traffic, and hurricane exposure.

### Guide Services

- **Cypress Cove Marina** (Venice) -- Best meet point for shuttle back from the Gulf.
- **Venice Marina** -- Alternate meet/shuttle point.
- Powerboat shuttles from the Gulf can be arranged with local fishing boats or workboats returning to Venice.
- **LiNKS (Leave No Kid on Shore)** -- Accepts vessel donations at journey's end. Contact: John Ruskey, Lower Mississippi River Foundation, Clarksdale, MS. Phone: 662-627-4070. Email: john@island63.com.
