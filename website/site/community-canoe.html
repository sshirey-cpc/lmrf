Community Canoe Trips

Join us for a day on the Mississippi River! Our Community Canoe Trips are monthly adventures to Mississippi River island beaches in voyageur canoes, presented in partnership with Quapaw Canoe Company. No previous experience necessary — everyone paddles together in our big canoes with experienced guides.

“A community that canoes together, moves together, grooves together, glues together: the balance of the canoe is a life lesson for balance, teamwork, and harmony.”
A Gift to the Community: No Charge!
Starting this month, for the next two months, we are offering a special gift to our community members (anyone who lives in the counties and states along the Lower Mississippi River Valley) -- no charge for Community Canoe in March and April 2026! Special offer -- especially for anyone who has never enjoyed the pleasure of paddling on the Mississippi River.

Free for Youth of All Ages. Free for Families. Free for Educators. Free for All Others.

This is our way of saying "thank you" for allowing us to serve you! Canoe trip is $110 value. Help us keep these opportunities alive by making a tax-deductible donation!

Trip Details

  • One Saturday each month (see schedule below)
  • Trips depart from Clarksdale, MS or Helena, AR
  • Duration: approximately 5 hours
  • All paddle together, no previous experience necessary
  • Must be willing to paddle and must wear life jacket
  • Reservations required. 18 max -- first come, first served

Quapaw Canoe Company Provides

Voyageur canoe, paddles, lifejackets and all necessary river gear, first aid kits, VHF Marine Radio, running lights, and all necessary emergency gear. We'll pack group water in 5-gallon jugs to refill your personal water bottles.

Pricing

Adults $50
Educators $25
Children under 18 Free (accompanied by parent or guardian)

What to Pack

In a daypack or drybag:

  • Water bottles
  • Snacks
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
  • Bug protection
  • Shoes that can get muddy and wet (or go barefoot)

Optional:

  • Cell phone, camera, bird book, binoculars
  • Swim gear and towel

Wear: Dress for possible rainy weather. Wear shoes that can get muddy and wet (or go barefoot).

Note: Pack any electronics in a dry bag, dry box, or zip lock bags.
